Product Overview The FS-QTP/Z integrated pipe network smart sensor (energy type) is a multifunctional data acquisition and control device designed specifically for heating and cooling hydraulic pipe network systems. This product integrates multiple measurement functions including flow rate, temperature, pressure, differential pressure, and energy, enabling holographic data collection and digital management of pipe networks. It solves the industry pain points of unsystematic data collection and asynchronous hydraulic balance control in traditional pipe network systems, providing a reliable hardware foundation for the refined operation of smart water and HVAC systems. ### 2. Key Features - Integrated multi-parameter acquisition (valve position, flow rate, differential pressure, temperature, temperature difference, energy) - Non-contact Bluetooth display and parameter setting - Unique exhaust and backwash design for accurate and durable measurement - Support for RS485 and Bluetooth dual communication interfaces - Modular design with pressure-replaceable key components - Remote data transmission and cloud platform access - Dynamic differential pressure balance control function - Anti-vibration and start-stop memory functions ### 3. Typical Applications #### Scenario 1: Hydraulic Balance Commissioning of Central Air Conditioning Systems In central air conditioning systems of large commercial buildings, FS-QTP/Z sensors can be installed on the supply and return water pipes of each floor and area to collect key parameters such as flow rate, temperature, and differential pressure in real time. Through algorithm analysis on the smart hydraulic platform, the system can automatically adjust the opening of electric control valves to achieve dynamic hydraulic balance of the entire pipe network. This not only significantly shortens system commissioning time but also effectively reduces pump energy consumption and improves the operating efficiency and indoor comfort of the air conditioning system. #### Scenario 2: Urban Central Heating Pipe Network Monitoring and Energy Efficiency Management In urban central heating systems, FS-QTP/Z sensors can be deployed at heat exchange stations, building entrances, and key nodes to comprehensively monitor the operating status of the heating pipe network. Data such as heat, flow rate, and temperature collected by the sensors are uploaded to the cloud platform in real time to generate energy efficiency analysis reports and fault warnings. Managers can optimize heating scheduling accordingly, promptly detect and handle pipe network leaks, blockages, and other issues, achieving full-cycle refined management of the heating system. ### 5.
